Flounder dish made with Pocari Sweat

Considering Pocari Sweat is a sports drink designed to replenish lost electrolytes, it has a sweet and slightly salty flavor that could potentially be used in a variety of ways when cooking. We can get inspiration from the various ways that Kool-Aid, a sweet powdered drink, is used in recipes. For example, Kool-Aid is used in pies, popcorn, and even fried food​1​​2​.

Given this, you could consider creating a Pocari Sweat reduction or glaze to accompany your flounder. Here's a potential way to do it:

Ingredients:

  • 1 whole flounder, cleaned
  • 2 cups of Pocari Sweat
  • Salt to taste
  • Pepper to taste
  • Olive oil

Instructions:

  1. Season the flounder with salt and pepper on both sides.
  2. Heat olive oil in a pan over medium heat. Once heated, add the flounder and cook until golden brown on both sides. Remove the flounder and set it aside.
  3. In the same pan, add the Pocari Sweat. Cook it over medium heat until it reduces to about half its original volume. The Pocari Sweat should become thicker and more syrupy.
  4. Once the Pocari Sweat has reduced, return the flounder to the pan and spoon the Pocari Sweat reduction over the fish. Cook for a few more minutes until the flounder is cooked through and has absorbed some of the Pocari Sweat flavor.
  5. Serve the flounder with the Pocari Sweat reduction drizzled on top.

This is just a starting point and you could adjust the recipe based on your personal taste preferences.

Please note that cooking with sports drinks is not a common practice and the flavor might not appeal to everyone. The sweetness and saltiness of the Pocari Sweat could result in a unique flavor profile that may or may not be desirable. I would recommend trying a small amount first to see if you like the combination of flavors.
